MY TRIP ACROSS THE INDIAN OCEAN

On the 26th of December, my family embarked on a 7-day cruise that will see us going to a little island called Reunion Island and spending the new year in Mauritius. As we boarded the ship, we were greeted by the friendly crew and a sea of smiling faces. We found our cabins and quickly settled in, unpacking our bags, and getting ready for the adventure ahead. The first day we spent just walking around the boat and seeing what it had in store for us. This boat had everything from swimming pools to a club on the 12th floor to a casino and lounge on the bottom floors.

I spent the rest of the night in a teenage gathering area just playing games and talking to people but then went to bed early because we had had a long trip to Durban where the cruise departed. We spent the day following day on the sea and that day was the day that I met some amazing people that I would later spend the whole cruise with.

The following morning, the ship arrived at Réunion Island. My family and I disembarked and eagerly explored the island's many wonders. We visited the Piton des Neiges, a dormant volcano that offers breathtaking views of the island. You also hiked through the lush rainforests, swam in crystal-clear waterfalls, and visited charming villages.

After a fun-filled day spent on Reunion Island, it was time for us to sail to Mauritius. We arrived in Mauritius on the 31st of December. When we arrived, we disembarked the ship with a handful of guests to explore this beautiful island. We went to the Pamplemousses Botanical Garden, and everything was just breathtaking. After that, we separated from the group and went to the Port Louis market to do some shopping. As the night drew near, we returned to the ship where we celebrated New Year’s on the deck of the ship as we saw the beautiful fireworks. The next morning the ship departed Mauritius and headed back to South Africa, and we just looked back at the island with feelings of not wanting the trip to end but as we all know, all good things come to an end and all we have left are just memories that no one can never take away from us.
